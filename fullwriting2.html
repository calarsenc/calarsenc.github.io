Scientific Progress towards WBE

Neuroscience

Traditional neuroscience methods that rely on testing correlations between behavior and brain activity cannot reverse engineer a microprocessor (Jonas and Kording 2017), they are therefore unlikely to achieve a true understanding of our much more complex system - the brain. As Feynman's adage goes:

“What I cannot create, I do not understand.” — Richard Feynman

However, thanks to the miracle of sustained economic growth and surplus, the heroic scientific efforts of millions, and data collection increasing exponentially over the last decade, we now have modern neuroscience methods of sufficient caliber that the field is just about able to move from correlational studies to causative explorations.

The Future

The bottom-up WBE approach requires recording the whole brain to have any chance, and this remains constant in terms of personal information theoretic continuation...

Although still several decades away, the logical final result of these neuroscience related efforts is Whole Brain Emulation, a functional one-to-one model of the brain. Unless our rates of progress collapse - we will achieve this goal. The possibilities, and the risks of WBE will then become real...
