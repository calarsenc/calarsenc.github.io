Growing Opportunities in Neuroscience

Neuroscience

Traditional neuroscience methods that rely on testing correlations between behavior and brain activity cannot reverse engineer a microprocessor (Jonas and Kording 2017), they are therefore unlikely to achieve a true understanding of our much more complex system - the brain. As Feynman's adage goes:

“What I cannot create, I do not understand.” — Richard Feynman

However, thanks to the miracle of sustained economic growth and surplus, the heroic scientific efforts of millions, and data collection increasing exponentially over the last decade, we now have modern neuroscience methods of sufficient caliber to move from correlational studies to causative explorations on a grand scale.

Modern Neuroscience methods now leverage:

  • Chemogenetics, Optogenetics, Photopharmacology, Calcium and voltage imagining to analyze functional interactions within neural circuits showing how neural codes are formed and transformed within the brain.
  • High fidelity datasets created with high throughput electron microscopy and high resolution optical expansion microscopy that provide comprehensive anatomical and morphological data.
  • Reconstructions into 3D connectomes using AI driven interpretation and human proofreading.
  • Identification and categorization of a growing number of cell types, and their components such as synaptic receptors and ion channels.
  • Engram labeling technology which is becoming increasingly capable, and experiencing a lot of valuable experimentation.

It is with methods such as this that we enter a new paradigm in neuroscience.

Virtual Neuroscience

Virtual neuroscience that enables causative exploration has several immediate advantages, including:

  • Fully Observable
  • Reproducible
  • Causal ablations, interventions, and edits
  • Differentiable
  • Less animals involved in research
  • Faster (Olah 2021)

    Space to Improve

    Interplay between the Math, Neuroscience, and ML fields is greatest in the burgeoning field of NeuroAI, and involves foundation models for neuroscience. And we already know that brain decoders can be built by learning the latent structure of neural data through supervised and unsupervised learning to an impressive degree. Neuro models, despite their simplicity and lack of fitted parameters, already effectively predict a wide range of cortical stimulation data (Fine, I., Boynton, G.M, 2024). These models act as 'virtual patients' or 'digital twins,' that are comparable to virtual prototypes in digital manufacturing. Like for any field that moves into digital engineering, such digital prototyping allows rapid and cheap iterative development (most of the things you see right now were created in this manner). This highlights the reasonable nature of being excited about growth in this field, every other engineering field is doing it or already has, BCI and Clinical Neurotech are likely to follow the same footsteps.     The Future:

    Although still several decades away, the logical final result of these neuroscience related efforts is Whole Brain Emulation, a functional one-to-one model of the brain. Unless our rates of progress collapse - we will achieve this goal.
    “For those of a physicalist, functionalist, and patternist persuasion concerning the mind, not only is mind uploading possible, it is inevitable” (Wiley 2023)
    The possibilities, and the risks of WBE will then become real. However skeptical such a technology may be perceived today, we must plan for it, and preservation may bridge the gap for those who cannot otherwise stay up to see the sunrise of this new age should it come to pass in the future.
